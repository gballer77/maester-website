Your team's documentation is only half the story — the other half lives in the framework docs, library READMEs, and reference sites your code actually depends on. Most of those upstream projects have no reason to ship a Maester config for you.

## The scenario

The libraries and frameworks your team depends on usually have excellent documentation — but it lives in *their* repository, on *their* terms, and they have no reason to publish a Maester config for downstream consumers. Without a way to pull selectively from those repos, your aggregated context stops at the edge of code you control.

## How this example handles it

Each source declares an `includes` list of glob patterns. Maester resolves the upstream repo, matches the globs against its tree, and pulls only the files you actually want — for instance:

- `src/content/docs/**/*.mdx` from the Astro docs
- `src/pages/docs/**/*.mdx` from the Tailwind site

Nothing else from those repositories enters the citadel.

## What it gets you

- **Canonical upstream references, on your terms.** AI agents and code search see the framework docs alongside your own, pinned to the version you choose.
- **No forks, no vendoring, no upstream PRs.** Pull what you need without asking the maintainers for cooperation.
- **One-line upgrades.** When a dependency moves, bumping the `ref` is the only change required to refresh its docs in your aggregation.

> Think of `includes` as a polite way to depend on someone else's documentation: you take only what you need, and you take it explicitly.
